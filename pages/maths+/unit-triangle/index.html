Trigonometric Ratios in a Unit Triangle
In trigonometry, the unit triangle, also known as the right-angled triangle with a hypotenuse of 1, provides a foundation for understanding key trigonometric ratios. Let's explore these relationships:
Sine (sin θ)
Sine is defined as the ratio of the length of the side opposite the angle (opposite side) to the hypotenuse. In the unit triangle, sin θ = opposite/hypotenuse. Therefore, sin θ = a/1 = a, where 'a' is the length of the opposite side.
Cosine (cos θ)
Cosine is defined as the ratio of the length of the side adjacent to the angle (adjacent side) to the hypotenuse. In the unit triangle, cos θ = adjacent/hypotenuse. Therefore, cos θ = b/1 = b, where 'b' is the length of the adjacent side.
Tangent (tan θ)
Tangent is defined as the ratio of the length of the side opposite the angle to the side adjacent to the angle. In the unit triangle, tan θ = opposite/adjacent. Therefore, tan θ = a/b, where 'a' is the length of the opposite side and 'b' is the length of the adjacent side.
Reciprocal Relationships
These trigonometric ratios establish fundamental relationships between the sides of a right-angled triangle and its angles. Understanding these ratios is essential for solving trigonometric problems and working with angles in various mathematical contexts.
