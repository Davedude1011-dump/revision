Graphing Inequalities
Graphing inequalities involves representing the solution sets of mathematical statements involving inequalities on a coordinate plane. Here are the general steps to graph inequalities:
1. Identify the Type of Inequality
Determine whether the inequality is linear or quadratic. The methods for graphing may vary based on the type.
2. Solve for the Boundary
For linear inequalities, solve the corresponding equation for the boundary line. For quadratic inequalities, determine the boundary by finding where the quadratic expression equals zero.
3. Choose Test Points
Select test points on either side of the boundary to determine the solution regions. Common choices are points (0,0), (1,0), and (0,1).
4. Represent the Solution Set
Use shading or a dotted line to represent the solution set on the appropriate side of the boundary. Solid lines indicate that points on the line are included in the solution set, while dotted lines mean the points on the line are not included.
5. Consider Inequalities with Two Variables
For inequalities involving two variables (x and y), graph the corresponding equation and use shading to represent the solution set based on the inequality sign.
Examples:

Consider the inequality y > 2x - 1. The steps involve graphing the line y = 2x - 1, choosing a test point, and shading the region above the line since it's a "greater than" inequality.
