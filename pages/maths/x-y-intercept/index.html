Finding X and Y Intercepts from an Equation
In algebra, you can find the x and y intercepts of a linear equation. The x-intercept is where the graph crosses the x-axis (y = 0), and the y-intercept is where the graph crosses the y-axis (x = 0). To find these intercepts, follow these steps:
Step 1: Finding the X-Intercept (y = 0)
To find the x-intercept, set y to 0 in the equation and solve for x:
1. Replace y with 0 in the equation.
2. Solve for x.
Step 2: Finding the Y-Intercept (x = 0)
To find the y-intercept, set x to 0 in the equation and solve for y:
1. Replace x with 0 in the equation.
2. Solve for y.
Example 1
Given the equation **2x - 3y = 6**, find the x and y intercepts.
Step 1: Finding the X-Intercept (y = 0)
1. Replace y with 0 in the equation:
**2x - 3(0) = 6**
2. Solve for x:
**2x = 6**
**x = 3**
So, the x-intercept is (3, 0).
Step 2: Finding the Y-Intercept (x = 0)
1. Replace x with 0 in the equation:
**2(0) - 3y = 6**
2. Solve for y:
**-3y = 6**
**y = -2**
So, the y-intercept is (0, -2).
Example 2
Given the equation **5x + 2y = 10**, find the x and y intercepts.
Step 1: Finding the X-Intercept (y = 0)
1. Replace y with 0 in the equation:
**5x + 2(0) = 10**
2. Solve for x:
**5x = 10**
**x = 2**
So, the x-intercept is (2, 0).
Step 2: Finding the Y-Intercept (x = 0)
1. Replace x with 0 in the equation:
**5(0) + 2y = 10**
2. Solve for y:
**2y = 10**
**y = 5**
So, the y-intercept is (0, 5).
Conclusion
Finding the x and y intercepts of an equation helps you understand where the graph of the equation crosses the x-axis and y-axis, respectively. These intercepts are valuable for graphing and analyzing linear equations.
