Perpendicular Lines
In geometry, two lines are said to be perpendicular when they intersect at a right angle (90 degrees). Perpendicular lines have distinctive properties and can be identified based on these characteristics.
Properties of Perpendicular Lines
1. Right Angle Intersection: Perpendicular lines intersect at a 90-degree angle, forming four right angles at their point of intersection.
2. Negative Reciprocal Slopes: The slopes (gradients) of two perpendicular lines are negative reciprocals of each other. If one line has a slope of **m**, the other line's slope is **-1/m**.
3. Product of Slopes: If you multiply the slopes of two lines that are perpendicular to each other, the result is always **-1**. This is another way to express the relationship between their slopes.
Identifying Perpendicular Lines
To determine if two lines are perpendicular, you can use the following methods:
1. Calculate Slopes: Find the slopes of both lines. If the product of their slopes is **-1**, the lines are perpendicular.
2. Use the Angle Criteria: If two lines intersect and form four right angles at the point of intersection, they are perpendicular.
3. Negative Reciprocal Slopes: Check if the slopes of the lines are negative reciprocals of each other. If they are, the lines are perpendicular.
Examples of Perpendicular Lines
Let's explore some examples of perpendicular lines:
Example 1: Determine if the lines with equations **y = 2x + 3** and **y = -1/2x - 1** are perpendicular.
1. Calculate Slopes:
The first line has a slope of **2**, and the second line has a slope of **-1/2**.
2. Product of Slopes:
Multiply the slopes: **(2) x (-1/2) = -1**. Since the product of the slopes is **-1**, the lines are perpendicular.
Example 2: Find the equation of the line that is perpendicular to **y = 3x + 2** and passes through the point **(4, 6)**.
1. Determine Slope:
The given line has a slope of **3**. A line perpendicular to it will have a negative reciprocal slope of **-1/3**.
2. Use Point-Slope Form:
The equation of the perpendicular line in point-slope form is: **y - 6 = -1/3(x - 4)**.
3. Solve for **y**:
**y - 6 = -1/3(x - 4)**
**y = -1/3(x - 4) + 6**
**y = -1/3x + 4/3 + 6**
**y = -1/3x + 22/3**
So, the equation of the perpendicular line passing through the point **(4, 6)** is **y = -1/3x + 22/3**.
Conclusion
Understanding perpendicular lines is crucial in geometry and algebra. Perpendicular lines not only intersect at right angles but also exhibit specific relationships between their slopes. Identifying these characteristics can help you determine if lines are perpendicular and find equations for perpendicular lines in various contexts.
