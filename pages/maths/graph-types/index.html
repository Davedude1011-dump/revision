Types of Graphs and Their Equations
Graphs represent mathematical relationships and can take various forms. Here are some major types:

Linear Graph
Equation: y = mx + b (where m is the slope and b is the y-intercept)

Quadratic Graph
Equation: y = ax^2 + bx + c (where a, b, and c are constants)

Cubic Graph
Equation: y = ax^3 + bx^2 + cx + d (where a, b, c, and d are constants)

Quartic Graph
Equation: y = ax^4 + bx^3 + cx^2 + dx + e (where a, b, c, d, and e are constants)
Reciprocal Graph
Equation: y = a/x (where a is a constant)

Square Root Graph
Equation: y = sqrt(x) (positive square root)

Cube Root Graph
Equation: y = cbrt(x)

Absolute Value Graph
Equation: y = |x| (absolute value)

Exponential Graph
Equation: y = a * b^x (where a and b are constants)

Logarithmic Graph
Equation: y = log_b(x) (where b is the base of the logarithm)

Trigonometric Graphs
Examples: y = sin(x), y = cos(x), y = tan(x)

Circle Graph
Equation: (x - h)^2 + (y - k)^2 = r^2 (center at (h, k))
These equations represent the basic forms of each type of graph, capturing a wide range of mathematical relationships.
