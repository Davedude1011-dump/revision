Chapter 1: Rationalizing Denominators with Single Surds
Rationalizing the denominator with a single surd involves removing the surd from the denominator. Here's how to do it:
Step 1: Identify the surd in the denominator, e.g., 1/√a.
Step 2: Multiply both the numerator and denominator by the conjugate of the surd, which is (√a).
Step 3: Simplify the expression to obtain the rationalized form, e.g., (√a)/a.
Example 1: Rationalize 1/√5.
Example 2: Rationalize 3/(√7).

Step 3: Simplify the expression to obtain the rationalized form, e.g., (√a - √b)/(a - b).
