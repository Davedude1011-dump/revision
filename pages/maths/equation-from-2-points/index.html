Finding the Equation of a Line from Two Points
When you are given two distinct points (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂) on a line, you can determine the equation of that line. The equation of a line, typically in slope-intercept form (y = mx + b), can be found using the following steps:
Step 1: Calculate the Slope (m)
The first step is to calculate the slope (m) of the line using the formula:
**m = (y₂ - y₁) / (x₂ - x₁)**
Where:
- (x₁, y₁) and (x₂, y₂) are the coordinates of the two points.
Now, let's work through examples to understand this process:
Example 1
Given two points A(2, 3) and B(5, 7), find the equation of the line passing through these points.
Step 1: Calculate the slope (m)
**m = (7 - 3) / (5 - 2) = 4 / 3**
Step 2: Use the Point-Slope Form
Now that we have the slope (m), we can use the point-slope form of the equation:
**y - y₁ = m(x - x₁)**
Using point A(2, 3), we substitute the values:
**y - 3 = (4/3)(x - 2)**
Step 3: Simplify to Slope-Intercept Form
To express the equation in slope-intercept form (y = mx + b), we simplify the equation:
**y = (4/3)x - 2/3**
So, the equation of the line passing through points A(2, 3) and B(5, 7) is **y = (4/3)x - 2/3**.
Example 2
Given points C(-1, 2) and D(3, -4), find the equation of the line through these points.
Step 1: Calculate the slope (m)
**m = (-4 - 2) / (3 - (-1)) = -6 / 4 = -3/2**
Step 2: Point-Slope Form
Using point C(-1, 2):
**y - 2 = (-3/2)(x - (-1))**
Step 3: Slope-Intercept Form
Simplifying the equation:
**y = (-3/2)x + 1**
So, the equation of the line through points C(-1, 2) and D(3, -4) is **y = (-3/2)x + 1**.
Conclusion
Finding the equation of a line from two points involves calculating the slope and then using the point-slope form to express it. This process allows you to determine the equation that describes the straight line passing through the given points. It's a fundamental concept used in various fields of mathematics and science.
